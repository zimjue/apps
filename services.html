This page describes the kind of independent iPhone and iPad collaborations I occasionally take on alongside my own apps. The best fit is a focused scope, clear user value, and a calm product direction.

Good fit

  • A clear app idea for iPhone or iPad
  • A digital product that should become a focused Apple app
  • A clearly scoped independent collaboration that values thoughtful UX and clean execution
  • A project where clarity, calmness, and product quality matter

Less ideal

  • Very large enterprise platforms with many stakeholders
  • Projects that need a full agency team across many channels
  • Apps with unclear scope, shifting priorities, or no product direction
  • Purely feature-heavy builds without a clear user problem

What you can expect from me

  • Clear product thinking instead of feature sprawl
  • A calm, readable Apple-first user experience
  • Honest feedback when something should be simplified
  • A practical path toward a shippable first version

What to include in your note

  • What the app should do in one or two sentences
  • Who it is for
  • Whether designs, brand assets, or an existing product already exist
  • Your rough timeline and what stage the idea is in
  • Your expected scope or budget range, if you already have one
